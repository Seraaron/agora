## Weapons

Weapons work a bit differently from [[Armor List|armor and shields]], and are more like _tools_ for the various #martial jobs. **They grant no inherent dice from materials or weight, and instead derive their bonus dice from production quality**.

#que This quality system assumes that you're using period-appropriate weapons and armor against one another in a battle. If someone is somehow using modern guns against iron age armor, for example, then your [[Facilitator|FC]] may grant a few bonus dice to the attacker to compensate.

### Unarmed Weapons

Unarmed or #natural weapons have no cost or quality because they are innate to your species. Their weights are only for used for combat moves — they do not count towards [[Carry Weight|encumbrance]] — and they cannot be disarmed either.

### Firearm Generations

#gunner and #firearm weapons are divided into six 'generations', according to the technology that was available to create them. The guns generally get more streamlined, in each generation, with fewer qualifying tags and drawbacks. That _would ordinarily_ make them cheaper, if they weren't also getting more powerful as represented through their inherent dice bonuses — equal to the generation number.

Which generation/s of gun you allow in your game will heavily depend on your game's [[setting and situation|setting]] and [[Genre, Tone, and Themes|tone]], so always discuss their inclusion with your [[Peers]] during [[Development Sessions|session zero]]. Be aware that any guns beyond the second generation may be considered 'overpowered' as they can break the normal [[Martial Combat|combat]] system pretty easily.

Each generation also contains [[Excellent-Quality|excellent-quality]] versions of all the guns in the previous generation, granting +1 bonus die. Though these are not listed, so you will have to calculate their costs manually.

#que 'Zeroth Gen' firearms are listed above, under the [[#CQ Projectiles|common-quality projectiles]].
